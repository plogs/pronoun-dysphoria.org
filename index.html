What is pronoun dysphoria?

Pronoun dysphoria is the condition typically associated with persons who identify with the use of old school pronouns (e.g., he, she, them, they).

Dysphoria can be a serious condition. Dysphoria means to be in a profound state of unease or dissatisfaction.

For example, "body integrity dysphoria" (or BID) is a mental condition wherein an otherwise normal-bodied person wants to be disabled in some way — such as amputating their leg or becoming blind

Pronoun dysphoria is not normally considered a mental condition, although the unease and dissatisfaction associated with the condition is real and often painful.


'gender recognition pins'

The enforced use of "preferred gender pronouns" can be extremely painful to a person who experiences pronoun dysphoria.

The use of 'gender recognition pins' is considered disrespectful to people who identify as pronoun dysphoric.
