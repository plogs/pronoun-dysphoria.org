What is pronoun dysphoria?

Pronoun dysphoria is the condition typically associated with persons who identify with the use of old school pronouns (e.g., he, she, them, they).

"What does that mean, identify with?"

"It means some people see the world in a certain way. In a traditional way. Which is to say, old school pronouns are the pronouns that make sense, given their world view. The use of new style pronouns is abhorrent to the pronoun dysphoric."

Dysphoria can be a serious condition. Dysphoria means to be in a profound state of unease or dissatisfaction.

For example, "body integrity dysphoria" (or BID) is a mental disorder characterized by a desire to have a sensory or physical disability or having discomfort with being able-bodied. It typically begins in early adolescence and may result in harmful consequences such as self-amputation. For example, one may desire to lose a leg or to become blind or deaf.

Pronoun dysphoria is not an extreme dysphoria like BID, but the unease and dissatisfaction associated with the condition is real and often painful.
